Chester Charles Bennington

Chester live in a concert.

Born on 20th March, 1976, in the state of Arizona, USA, he was a musician.
He performed in these bands:

  • Linken Park
  • Dead by Sunrise
  • Grey Daze
  • Stone temple pilots

His most successful band was Linkin Park. On July 20, 2017, Bennington was found dead at his home
in Palos Verdes Estates, California; his death was ruled a suicide by hanging.
The band released some revolutionary albums.

Hybrid Theory

Linkin Park released Hybrid Theory on October 24, 2000.
The album, which represented half a decade's worth of the band's work.
Hybrid Theory was a massive commercial success; it sold more than 4.8 million
copies during its debut year, earning it the status of best-selling album of 2001.

Meteora

Linkin Park's second album debuted on March 25, 2003 and instantly
earned worldwide recognition, going to No. 1 in the US and UK, and No. 2 in Australia.
Meteora earned the band multiple awards and honors. The band won the MTV awards
for Best Rock Video for "Somewhere I Belong" and the Viewer's Choice Award for "Breaking the Habit".

Minutes to Midnight

Minutes to Midnight is the third studio album by American rock band Linkin Park,
released on May 14, 2007, through Warner Bros. Records.

A Thousand Suns

In May 2009, Linkin Park announced they were working on a fourth studio album,
which was planned to be released in 2010. Shinoda told IGN that the new album would
be 'genre-busting,' while building off of elements in Minutes to Midnight.
He also mentioned that the album would be more experimental and "hopefully more cutting-edge".

One More Light

Linkin Park began working on new material for a seventh studio album in November 2015.
Chester Bennington commented on the album's direction by stating,
"We've got a lot of great material that I hope challenges our fanbase
as well as inspires them as much as it has us."
